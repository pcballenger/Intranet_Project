Brandeis School of Law

The Brandeis School of Law Information Technology Department's three full-time staff support our community's use of desktop, portable, instructional and communications technologies. For students, this includes exams on computer, computer labs and printing, and classroom and other audiovisual technology. For faculty and staff, this includes dedicated support and training, instructional and A-V technology, hardware and software purchasing and management, and information security.

Mission Statement
The University of Louisville Brandeis School of Law Information Technology Department strives to provide the law school with technology and support that enable it to operate in furtherance of its mission and its program of legal education. This includes infrastructure and security, training, and hardware, software and communication resources that enhance the teaching, scholarship, research, service and administrative needs of the law school's students, faculty and staff.

Adapted from Chapter 7, ABA Standards and Rules of Procedure for Approval of Law Schools.
